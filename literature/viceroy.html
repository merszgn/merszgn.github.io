Viceroy

Work Uploaded: March 2, 2025.

A butterfly and a silkworm walk into a bar. The arrogance leaks from the silkworm's mind, its exocarp seeping with envious rage. Our butterfly; naively unaware of its jealousy approaches hesitantly. Its wings, translucent and glittering in warm sunlight, welcome the awful silkworm to wordless conversation. Of course, how should two species of no relevance ever communicate in any oral exchange? And so, the butterfly flaps its gentle wings and the silkworm responds with aggressive waves of its many legs.

What is its purpose?, ruminates the silkworm. It does not spin silk, nor does it live long enough to make any difference. A solely aesthetic creature. What a waste of air and matter, scoffs the silkworm.

What an ugly creature!, ruminates the butterfly. No being would ever praise a worm suchlike they do unto me. Its existence brings no joy or beauty. What a waste of air and matter, scoffs the butterfly.

So, with no words spoken, the two part ways with a kind, warm salute. A goodbye with ill intention; an intangible concept made for neither to comprehend. With no notice of one another's true impressions, they leave the cedar bar. In the end, what purpose did their hatred aid? They are separate forms of the same being, unknown to them. A heart bathed in neglect and fear is no heart. An ardor for fruitlessness is no ardor. Their intrinsic metamorpheses will prove one day.
