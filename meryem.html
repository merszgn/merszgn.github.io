Pomegranatalia

My favorite fruit has consistently been pomegranates. In a verbatim sense, the red juice and crunch of its seeds fills an aperture in my body that I forget exists. Metaphorically–yet so literally–, this fruit is my soul. One can husk the outer shell to get to the sweet core; the memories, the psyche, and the accumulation of human emotion. Its sole existence is a manifestation of human life. Unlike other fruit, it offers knowledge. Solitaries suchlike seedless grapes and coconuts only embody humanity of a trivial scale and a vain confidence beyond inferior comprehension. But what does a pomegranate suggest? Does it personify all aspects of extant life or detach the virtuous and heinous into conventional classes?

My mind is a disarray of perplexity and complicated thoughts that I cannot elucidate to most others. I struggle to find the words to illustrate my habits whilst my peers make sense of the world simply by being. I cannot find it in myself to do the same. I have tried. I have attempted to sit and understand the world through simply… understanding it. Telling myself the complexity is fabricated and present to deceive me. Perhaps it is true, perhaps it is not. Regardless, I am unable to relate to this approach of reasoning. I have succumbed and accepted that my brain just does not experience and make sense of things the way that others’ do. I search for the intricacy in everything and lose myself in the process. One day, I recall becoming so weary of hauling around this hunger for clarity in an uncertain world. I wanted to stop pinning the blame of being so burdensome onto myself and needed to channel the energy into something else, something that could understand everything without saying anything. And in that moment, in front of me on the table, was a bowl of pomegranates. They heaped up onto each other and possessed vibrant hues of distinct shades of red. The skin bruised and coarse, speckled and imperfect. The core attached together by intricate webs of membrane and seeds comprising the bliss and the mold of its fruit. What a perfectly imperfect manifestation of what it means to be human. So, I did what I had to and I began to condemn the heaviness of existing onto pomegranates. It began as a senseless bit; I would go through agonizing misery and convince myself it was the pomegranates’ fault. Everything terrible happened because of the pomegranates. After, I would consume their seeds and swallow the sadness to dissolve in my stomach into utter nothingness. The world started to make sense through this minor delusion of mine. Eventually, it ceased being a senseless bit. I began to genuinely have myself convinced that all my unluckiness and fortune were results of the existence of pomegranates. However, this dilemma was not all that tricky. The complexity within it was the psychological factor affecting me. At this point of my awkward, perturbing self-experiment, even the slightest emotions brought the scent of pomegranates to my senses. The dry smell of sweet, mouth-watering seeds would relentlessly follow me anywhere I went. Over time, I became increasingly obsessed with this peculiar vision of pomegranates I had established in my head. I suppose it was a result of finally having an outlet that helped me grasp the world. I was too afraid to let go of this idea now, too afraid to revert back to my numbed lack of realism. It was like an addiction consuming my brain. Everything traced back to pomegranates in one way or another.

The idea of the pomegranate became an intangible concept. The fruit no longer existed to me; it was as if the symbolism of it was the true definition of the word. I had gone so far into my delusion that pomegranates seemed like an invention I came up with myself. It was my brain. I thought of it in this sense. My head was a pomegranate. My skin and skull being the red shell of the fruit. My brain was the seeds. Each seed contains memories, nightmares, dreams, voices, photographs, entries: everything. I am unsure around what point in this timeline that I had started to think this way. I did not care to remember, either. It was enough for me that my brain had calmed down at last. I no longer think of questions with no answers. I no longer become frustrated with the variables of the universe. I focus on what I know, and what I know is that I am okay. In all transparency, it is quite an underwhelming story to tell. I feel that I miss many components of how I came to process the world in this way. I guess those components have become swallowed seeds. I have forgotten them and they have dissolved in stomach acid.
