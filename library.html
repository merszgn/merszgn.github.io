Book of the Month

"The Personal Librarian tells the story of an extraordinary woman, famous for her intellect, style, and wit, and shares the lengths she must go to—for the protection of her family and her legacy—to preserve her carefully crafted white identity in the racist world in which she lives."

The Personal Librarian by Marie Benedict and Victoria Christopher Murray tells the true story of Belle da Costa Greene, the brilliant and ambitious woman who became the personal librarian to J.P. Morgan, curating one of the most prestigious collections of rare books and art. But beneath her success, Belle hides a powerful secret—she is a Black woman passing as white in a racially divided America. The novel explores themes of identity, sacrifice, and the cost of keeping secrets, showing how Belle had to navigate high society while concealing her true heritage to secure opportunities that would have been denied to her otherwise.
